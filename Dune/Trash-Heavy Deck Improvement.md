# Trash-Heavy Deck Improvement

Trash-heavy games are games where deck thinning becomes a central plan rather than a side benefit.

Bludgeon's trash-focused videos highlight a key tension: Dune: Imperium rewards a cleaner deck, but trashing is not free. Every trash action must justify the lost tempo.

## Why Trashing Matters

Trashing weak cards improves:

- Draw consistency.
- Reveal quality.
- Access to key cards.
- [[Dune/Persuasion|Persuasion]] reliability.
- The chance of seeing high-impact cards before the game ends.

This matters most when your deck has a clear target: [[Dune/The Spice Must Flow|The Spice Must Flow]], faction access, combat bursts, or expansion-specific synergies like [[Dune/Immortality|Immortality]] research and graft.

## When Trashing Is Strong

- You are removing genuinely weak cards.
- Your deck already contains cards worth finding more often.
- The trash effect is attached to an action you wanted anyway.
- You still have enough icons for [[Dune/Agents and Worker Placement|agent turns]].
- The game has enough rounds left for the cleaner deck to matter.

## When Trashing Is A Trap

- You trash instead of scoring.
- You thin away useful icons.
- You buy replacement cards that are only slightly better.
- You spend the whole middle game improving a deck that never converts.

## Practical Heuristic

Trash with a destination. A cleaner deck is not the point. A cleaner deck that buys [[Dune/The Spice Must Flow|The Spice Must Flow]], wins conflicts, reaches faction spaces, or executes a leader plan is the point.
