# Guild Bank and Economy-Centric Games

Economy-centric games are games where the winner is likely to be the player who converts [[Dune/Solari|solari]], [[Dune/Spice|spice]], shipping tempo, or money-like card effects into points more efficiently than the table.

The Bludgeon video **Take It To The BANK** is a useful example because the early game immediately revolves around a Guild Bank-style economy card, [[Dune/Swordmaster|Swordmaster]] timing, solari rewards, spice access, and whether economy can become actual scoring pressure.

## What To Watch

- Does the economy card create points, or only more resources?
- Can the extra solari become [[Dune/Swordmaster|Swordmaster]], [[Dune/High Council|High Council]], Mentat, or a decisive board action?
- Can the extra spice become [[Dune/Highliner|Highliner]], [[Dune/The Spice Must Flow|The Spice Must Flow]], tech, or combat pressure?
- Is the table letting one player keep both economy and tempo uncontested?
- Are you buying support cards because they fit the economy plan, or because they look efficient in isolation?

## Strategic Pattern

Economy-heavy games reward restraint. A large economy is only good if it converts on schedule. Bludgeon's recurring point across economy games is that money-like advantages need a route: early tempo, faction pressure, conflict dominance, card quality, or repeatable point buys.

If the economy player is not yet scoring, pressure their conversion points rather than their resource pile. Block the spaces that turn resources into victory points.

## Common Traps

- Treating resource income as a score lead.
- Buying too many cards because you can afford them.
- Delaying points while building an economy that will not pay back before the [[Dune/Game Phase Identification|late game]].
- Ignoring an opponent's six-spice [[Dune/Highliner|Highliner]] threat.
