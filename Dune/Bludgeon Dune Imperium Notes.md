# Bludgeon Dune Imperium Notes

Bludgeon is a Dune: Imperium-focused YouTube creator. His transcripts are useful because they focus on actual digital-game decision points rather than generic board-game advice.

## Basic Strategy Video Takeaways

- Early Swordmaster is a major power boost.
- Bludgeon's sample opening emphasizes **Wealth** and **Secure Contract** as a path to an early Swordmaster.
- The sample benchmark is getting Swordmaster by round 2 when the draw and spaces support it.
- High Council improves reveal turns and supports high-persuasion scoring.
- High Council and The Spice Must Flow belong together: if your deck can reveal for 9 persuasion, the point buy is usually the reason you paid for the Council seat.
- Card quality matters more than simply buying more cards.
- Bonus agent effects are important because they let one worker-placement action produce multiple outputs.
- Faction access is a key purchase priority because faction spaces lead to influence and points.
- Power Play is highlighted as a powerful access and influence card.
- CHOAM Directorship and Shifting Allegiances are called out as important influence tools.
- The Spice Must Flow is a major scoring route for persuasion-focused decks.
- Do not confuse infrastructure with victory. You do not win by merely having Swordmaster, High Council, or a pile of spice; those pieces must become points.
- Conflicts should be evaluated by reward quality, not by whether you can throw troops in.
- Conflict III begins around round 7, and those conflicts can justify serious troop and spice preparation.
- Highliner is a common late conflict lever, so six spice can matter more than it appears a round earlier.
- Troops are limited; check opponent troop supply, reveal swords, turn order, and combat intrigue risk before committing.
- Intrigue cards matter because they create hidden point and combat swings.
- Holding more than three intrigue cards can itself become a liability if opponents can steal or punish them.

## Rise of Ix Opening Video Takeaways

- Opening moves should be chosen with late-game scoring in mind.
- Solari planning matters because Swordmaster timing matters.
- Smuggling and shipping can become contested early lanes.
- Player order and blocking change what openings are realistic.
- In Ix games, shipping, dreadnoughts, tech, and point conversion need to be planned together.
- Moving to Smuggling before opponents can matter because blocking changes the whole early solari plan.
- The opening shipping line can produce a round-2 Swordmaster, and if you buy Swordmaster during an agent turn you can use the extra agent immediately.
- If opponents race the same solari/shipping lane, a water-first opening can be a legitimate pivot rather than a failed plan.
- If your first draw contains both daggers, the round-2 conflict can become more realistic, but only after you check opponents' reveal turns.
- If pursuing a conflict-heavy Ix plan, both dreadnoughts can become part of the mid-game objective.
- If pursuing an influence/persuasion plan, deck-building and point purchases matter more than winning every fight.
- Tech should be evaluated as a scoring and plan enabler, not as a novelty buy.

## Card, Tech, and Leader Ranking Corpus

- Leader strength is contextual. Rank leaders by whether their abilities create reliable scoring routes in the format you are playing, not by raw flash.
- Spice is flexible because it can become solari, Highliner pressure, card effects, tech, or point routes; early spice matters most when it has a conversion plan.
- Influence is precious in base/classic Dune: Imperium because the faction-track points and alliance races are central and not always easy to access.
- Trashing is valuable because the starting deck contains weak cards, but trashing is only worth the tempo if the cleaner deck has a scoring lane.
- Card rankings repeatedly separate access, reveal value, combat value, and deck consistency. A card can be excellent in one lane and mediocre in another.
- Mentat-style extra-agent effects can be valuable not only for another action, but also for delaying reveal until opponents commit.
- Ix tech should be bought for a specific job: scoring, influence swing, shipping support, combat leverage, or deck acceleration.
- Ix Imperium-card evaluation changes because shipping, tech, and dreadnoughts create payoffs that do not exist in base-only games.
- Immortality card evaluation revolves around research access, graft reliability, trashing, specimens, and whether the card improves the deck fast enough.

## Uprising Primer Takeaways

- Uprising should be learned as a system update, not as a small rules patch.
- Each player starts with three spies, and spies sit on observation posts until used.
- Infiltrate lets you recall a connected spy to access an occupied connected space.
- Gather intelligence and infiltrate compete for the same spy timing; do not assume one spy can pay for multiple effects.
- Spy agent icons can access spaces connected to spies without recalling the spy.
- Contracts create separate tactical objectives and change the value of spaces, cards, and economy lines.
- Completed contracts remain relevant because some cards care about how many you have finished.
- Sandworms reshape combat because they add strength and can double rewards.
- Maker Hooks are a prerequisite planning point for worm turns.
- Shield Wall timing matters because it affects worm deployment.
- Battle icons, objective cards, and new board spaces change what a "normal" Dune: Imperium turn is worth.
- High Council remains familiar but has extra visit value; Swordmaster is cheaper after the first player buys it.
- Mentat is replaced by Imperial Spy, which changes short-term extra-agent tactics.
- Mixing Uprising with Ix or Immortality needs separate evaluation because the modules push different incentives.
- In Uprising plus Ix, some Ix spaces are blocked or adapted around contracts.
- Bludgeon explicitly rates Bloodlines as the best expansion fit for Uprising in the primer.

## Bloodlines Corpus Takeaways

- Bloodlines should be evaluated separately for base Dune: Imperium and Uprising.
- Sardaukar commanders make combat planning more persistent; they are not just one-round troop boosts.
- Bloodlines adds more contracts and contract-adjacent incentives, so contract completion becomes a larger strategic lane.
- The expansion adds cards and tech that can affect combat, trashing, intrigue, and faction pressure; evaluate each by whether it supports your existing route.
- Bludgeon is more positive on Bloodlines as an Uprising companion than as a universal must-add for original Dune: Imperium.

## How To Use These Notes

- Prioritize concrete conversion paths to points.
- Think from the endgame backward.
- Improve card quality and access.
- Contest combat only when the reward is worth it.
- Re-evaluate standard openings by leader, turn order, visible conflict, and Imperium row.
- In expansion games, identify which module is actually driving the table: shipping, research, contracts, worms, or ordinary faction pressure.

Do not treat any opening as automatic. Leader, player order, conflict card, Imperium row, and expansion modules can all change the correct line.
