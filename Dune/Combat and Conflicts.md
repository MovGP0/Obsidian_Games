# Combat and Conflicts

Combat is an auction for the current Conflict card rewards and one of the main scoring lanes in [[Dune/Strategy Overview|Strategy Overview]].

[[Dune/Resource Economy|Troops]], swords, [[Dune/Intrigue Cards|intrigue cards]], [[Dune/Rise of Ix|dreadnoughts]], [[Dune/Uprising|worms]], and reveal timing can all change the auction.

## Before Committing

Ask:

- Is first place worth the troop spend?
- Is second or third place enough?
- Who has not revealed yet?
- How many swords can those players still reveal?
- Who might have Combat intrigue?
- Does anyone have more than three intrigue cards and therefore a steal/punish liability?
- Does this conflict award a victory point?
- Will I need these troops for a later, better conflict?

## Efficient Combat

Good combat wins rewards with the minimum necessary commitment. Bad combat wins a small reward by emptying your garrison.

If a conflict is not worth winning, use it to:

- Pick up a lower reward cheaply.
- Force someone else to overspend.
- Preserve troops for a later point conflict.

## Timing Notes

Bludgeon's basic strategy transcript calls out the jump in conflict stakes around **round 7**, when Conflict III cards begin. From that point on, combat is less about incidental rewards and more about whether a player can convert [[Dune/Troops|troops]], swords, intrigue, and [[Dune/Spice|spice]] into points.

[[Dune/Highliner|Highliner]] is one of the biggest [[Dune/Game Phase Identification|late-game]] conflict levers. Treat six spice as a tactical threshold: if a point conflict is coming, the player with six spice and enough troops can often threaten a much larger combat swing than their board position suggests.

Before you spend troops early, remember that troops are a finite late-game resource. A cheap early reward is not cheap if it leaves you unable to contest the first decisive Conflict III.

## Uprising Note

In Uprising, worms add combat strength and can double conflict rewards, so combat valuation changes dramatically. A conflict that is mediocre without worms may become decisive with them.

Do not count worm turns only by visible troops. Check whether the player has Maker Hooks, whether the Shield Wall permits the worm line, and whether the reward is worth doubling.
