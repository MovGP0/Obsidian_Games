# Resource Economy

Dune: Imperium has several currencies. Each is useful only when it points toward scoring; see [[Dune/Core Strategic Principles|Core Strategic Principles]] for the broader conversion rule.

## [[Dune/Solari|Solari]]

[[Dune/Solari|Solari]] buys [[Dune/Swordmaster|Swordmaster]], [[Dune/High Council|High Council]], Mentat access, and some [[Dune/Agents and Worker Placement|board effects]].

Early solari is often about getting Swordmaster. Later solari is about turning agent turns into higher-value actions.

## [[Dune/Spice|Spice]]

[[Dune/Spice|Spice]] fuels expensive military and political plays, including [[Dune/Highliner|Highliner]]-style [[Dune/Combat and Conflicts|combat]] turns and [[Dune/The Spice Must Flow|The Spice Must Flow]] purchases in the base game economy.

Spice is strong when you already know how it becomes points. Hoarding spice without a conversion plan is usually weak.

## [[Dune/Water|Water]]

[[Dune/Water|Water]] opens desert spaces and card draw. Water is often not valuable by itself; it is valuable because it unlocks spice, cards, or troop deployment.

## [[Dune/Persuasion|Persuasion]]

[[Dune/Persuasion|Persuasion]] buys [[Dune/Deck Building and Card Quality|cards]] and [[Dune/The Spice Must Flow|The Spice Must Flow]]. A deck that reliably reaches high persuasion can score through repeated purchases, especially with [[Dune/High Council|High Council]] support.

## [[Dune/Troops|Troops]]

[[Dune/Troops|Troops]] are a resource you spend in [[Dune/Combat and Conflicts|conflict]]. Do not spend them just because you can deploy them. Spend them when the reward justifies the opportunity cost.
