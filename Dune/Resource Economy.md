# Resource Economy

Dune: Imperium has several currencies. Each is useful only when it points toward scoring.

## Solari

Solari buys Swordmaster, High Council, Mentat access, and some board effects.

Early solari is often about getting Swordmaster. Later solari is about turning agent turns into higher-value actions.

## Spice

Spice fuels expensive military and political plays, including Highliner-style combat turns and The Spice Must Flow purchases in the base game economy.

Spice is strong when you already know how it becomes points. Hoarding spice without a conversion plan is usually weak.

## Water

Water opens desert spaces and card draw. Water is often not valuable by itself; it is valuable because it unlocks spice, cards, or troop deployment.

## Persuasion

Persuasion buys cards and The Spice Must Flow. A deck that reliably reaches high persuasion can score through repeated purchases, especially with High Council support.

## Troops

Troops are a resource you spend in conflict. Do not spend them just because you can deploy them. Spend them when the reward justifies the opportunity cost.
