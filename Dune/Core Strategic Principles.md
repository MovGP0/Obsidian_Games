# Core Strategic Principles

## Points Are Scarce

Most [[Dune/Resource Economy|resources]] are only useful because they become points later. [[Dune/Spice|Spice]], [[Dune/Solari|solari]], [[Dune/Water|water]], [[Dune/Troops|troops]], [[Dune/Deck Building and Card Quality|cards]], and [[Dune/Intrigue Cards|intrigue]] are not goals by themselves.

Track where your next 3 points might come from. If you cannot name them, your current economy is probably unfocused.

## Agent Turns Are the Real Bottleneck

You have few [[Dune/Agents and Worker Placement|agent turns]]. A strong deck does not help if it gives you icons at the wrong time or sends you to low-impact spaces.

Prioritize:

- Getting a third agent through [[Dune/Swordmaster|Swordmaster]].
- Building hands that can reach faction spaces.
- Gaining draw so you can see more of your deck before reveal.
- Avoiding dead icons.

## Card Quality Beats Card Quantity

Adding too many mediocre cards makes it harder to draw the cards that actually open scoring lines.

Good cards usually do at least one of these:

- Open faction access.
- Provide draw or trashing.
- Generate resources on agent turns.
- Create combat bursts.
- Provide enough [[Dune/Persuasion|persuasion]] to support [[Dune/The Spice Must Flow|The Spice Must Flow]].
- Carry expansion-specific synergy.

## Conflicts Are Auctions

[[Dune/Combat and Conflicts|Conflict]] is not "fight because you can." It is an auction for rewards.

Before committing troops, ask:

- What reward am I actually buying?
- Who can still add combat after me?
- Do I need first place, or is second enough?
- Is this conflict worth losing troops that could win a later point conflict?

## Think From the Endgame Backward

Bludgeon's opening advice repeatedly points back to late-game scoring. Do not choose an opening because it is generically strong. Choose it because it makes a plausible final score path.

Examples:

- [[Dune/Swordmaster|Swordmaster]] opening: I am buying more agent turns and will use them for faction pressure, economy, or conflict.
- [[Dune/High Council|High Council]] opening: I am making repeated high-persuasion reveals and likely buying [[Dune/The Spice Must Flow|The Spice Must Flow]].
- Conflict opening: I am building troop flow, combat cards, and timing for point conflicts.
- Ix shipping opening: I am using the shipping track to convert tempo into solari, spice, tech, troops, or points.
- Immortality opening: I am using research, trashing, graft, or Tleilaxu access to create a stronger deck and point route.
- Uprising opening: I am building around spies, contracts, objectives, or worm-enabled combat.
