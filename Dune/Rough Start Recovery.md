# Rough Start Recovery

A rough start is not automatically fatal. It becomes fatal when you keep playing the opening you wanted instead of the position you actually have.

Bludgeon's **Can I Recover From A Rough Start?** is a good recovery case because the early turns include blocked plans, missed sequencing, and a forced pivot into whatever scoring lanes remain live.

## Recovery Priorities

1. Stop chasing the failed opener.
2. Identify the cheapest next point.
3. Preserve access to at least one real scoring lane.
4. Use [[Dune/Intrigue Cards|intrigue]], [[Dune/Faction Tracks and Alliances|alliances]], or [[Dune/Combat and Conflicts|combat]] to regain leverage.
5. Avoid desperation buys that make the deck worse.

## What To Count

- Who is already ahead on [[Dune/Swordmaster|Swordmaster]] or [[Dune/High Council|High Council]]?
- Which faction points are still realistic?
- Can you win a lower conflict reward cheaply?
- Can a delayed [[Dune/Highliner|Highliner]] or [[Dune/The Spice Must Flow|The Spice Must Flow]] route still score?
- Is your current hand bad, or is your next shuffle still salvageable?

## Recovery Mindset

The goal is not to recreate a perfect opening. The goal is to make your next two points cheaper than your opponents expect. A rough start often means you must accept second-best lines: defensive alliance pressure, opportunistic conflict rewards, or a narrower deck plan.
