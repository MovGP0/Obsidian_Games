# Harkonnen

You win through treachery-card volume, intimidation, and traitor pressure.

Harkonnen is strongest **early**, when fresh traitor uncertainty and double-card acquisition make the whole table afraid of you. But fear does not pay shipping bills.

Your plan is not "fight everything." It is:

> Fight the battles that strip hands, test leader safety, or produce captures.

Bid above market for genuinely important cards, but never so high that you land on CHOAM charity. Midgame Harkonnen often wins less through revealed traitors than through the **threat** of traitors, which pins opposing good leaders.

Late game, keep pressure asymmetric. Punish overextended factions and avoid equal-value slugfests you cannot finance twice.

Basic plan:

- Build a dangerous hand.
- Make opponents afraid to use their best leaders.
- Bid aggressively, but not ruinously.
- Exploit uncertainty around traitors.
- Pick fights that strip hands, test leaders, or create stronghold pressure.
