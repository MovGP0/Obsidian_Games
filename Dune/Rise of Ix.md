# Rise of Ix

Rise of Ix is an expansion for [[Dune/Dune Imperium Base Game|base Dune: Imperium]].

## What It Adds

- Interstellar Shipping and the freighter track.
- Tech tiles.
- Dreadnoughts.
- CHOAM board changes.
- New leaders, cards, intrigue, and conflicts.

## Strategic Shift

Shipping becomes a major [[Dune/Resource Economy|economy]] and tempo lane. [[Dune/Bludgeon Dune Imperium Notes|Bludgeon's Ix opening transcript]] emphasizes racing to useful [[Dune/Solari|solari]] and shipping positions, especially when trying to reach [[Dune/Swordmaster|Swordmaster]] quickly.

## Practical Notes

- Interstellar Shipping can become a central contested space.
- The shipping opener can support a round-2 [[Dune/Swordmaster|Swordmaster]], and a Swordmaster bought during an agent turn can be used immediately.
- If the shipping or [[Dune/Solari|solari]] lane is blocked, pivoting to [[Dune/Water|water]] can be better than fighting over a crowded plan.
- Dreadnoughts make [[Dune/Combat and Conflicts|conflict]] commitment more durable.
- Tech tiles create [[Dune/Deck Building and Card Quality|build-around]] incentives.
- The expansion rewards planning your [[Dune/Late Game Checklist|late-game point route]] earlier than the base game.

## Watch For

Do not chase shipping only because it is strong. It must convert into points, cards, tech, combat leverage, or faction pressure.

If you pursue a conflict-heavy Ix plan, Bludgeon's transcript points toward getting both dreadnoughts as part of the mid-game package. If you pursue an influence or [[Dune/Persuasion|persuasion]] plan, dreadnoughts are less central than faction access, [[Dune/High Council|High Council]] math, and [[Dune/The Spice Must Flow|The Spice Must Flow]].

## Tech Evaluation

Bludgeon's Ix tech-ranking transcript treats tech as plan-specific. A tech tile is good when it solves a concrete problem:

- Scoring or endgame conversion.
- Influence swing or faction access.
- Shipping-track acceleration.
- Combat leverage, especially with dreadnoughts.
- Deck speed, trashing, or card draw.

Do not buy tech merely because the discount is available. A discounted tech that does not support your route is still a spent action and a delayed point.
