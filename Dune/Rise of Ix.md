# Rise of Ix

Rise of Ix is an expansion for base Dune: Imperium.

## What It Adds

- Interstellar Shipping and the freighter track.
- Tech tiles.
- Dreadnoughts.
- CHOAM board changes.
- New leaders, cards, intrigue, and conflicts.

## Strategic Shift

Shipping becomes a major economy and tempo lane. Bludgeon's Ix opening transcript emphasizes racing to useful solari and shipping positions, especially when trying to reach Swordmaster quickly.

## Practical Notes

- Interstellar Shipping can become a central contested space.
- The shipping opener can support a round-2 Swordmaster, and a Swordmaster bought during an agent turn can be used immediately.
- If the shipping or solari lane is blocked, pivoting to water can be better than fighting over a crowded plan.
- Dreadnoughts make conflict commitment more durable.
- Tech tiles create build-around incentives.
- The expansion rewards planning your late-game point route earlier than the base game.

## Watch For

Do not chase shipping only because it is strong. It must convert into points, cards, tech, combat leverage, or faction pressure.

If you pursue a conflict-heavy Ix plan, Bludgeon's transcript points toward getting both dreadnoughts as part of the mid-game package. If you pursue an influence or persuasion plan, dreadnoughts are less central than faction access, High Council math, and The Spice Must Flow.

## Tech Evaluation

Bludgeon's Ix tech-ranking transcript treats tech as plan-specific. A tech tile is good when it solves a concrete problem:

- Scoring or endgame conversion.
- Influence swing or faction access.
- Shipping-track acceleration.
- Combat leverage, especially with dreadnoughts.
- Deck speed, trashing, or card draw.

Do not buy tech merely because the discount is available. A discounted tech that does not support your route is still a spent action and a delayed point.
