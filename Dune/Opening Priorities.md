# Opening Priorities

Bludgeon's basic-strategy transcript emphasizes the same early idea repeatedly: get your economy online quickly, especially the third agent, then convert that tempo into points.

## Default Priorities

1. Get Swordmaster early if the game state allows it.
2. Build toward a High Council seat if your deck wants The Spice Must Flow.
3. Add cards that improve faction access or hand quality.
4. Contest only the conflicts that are actually worth the troop spend.
5. Watch which players are racing the same faction tracks or solari spaces.

## Swordmaster

Swordmaster is usually the biggest early tempo upgrade because it gives a third agent for the rest of the game.

Bludgeon's basic opening example uses early **Wealth** and **Secure Contract** turns to reach Swordmaster quickly. In Ix, his opening discussion shows a shipping/solari line that can buy Swordmaster on round 2, with the important bonus that a Swordmaster bought during an agent turn can be used immediately.

Early Swordmaster is strongest when:

- You can reach 8 solari without derailing scoring.
- Your hand can support three meaningful agent turns.
- You are not giving up an immediate point to chase it.

It is weaker when:

- You have no useful icons.
- The table is already moving into point conflicts.
- Your leader or expansion module gives another efficient path.
- The solari path is heavily blocked and a water, faction, or card-quality opening produces a cleaner next two rounds.

## High Council

High Council is strongest when you expect to buy The Spice Must Flow or expensive cards. It is not automatic; if your deck cannot generate point buys, the seat may be too slow.

## Opening Pivots

Do not force a scripted opener after the table blocks it. In Ix games, if everyone is rushing the same shipping or solari line, Bludgeon's transcript treats water-first development as a real alternative. The right opening is the one that creates a live scoring route by the middle game.
