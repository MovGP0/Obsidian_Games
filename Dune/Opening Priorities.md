# Opening Priorities

Bludgeon's basic-strategy transcript emphasizes the same early idea repeatedly: get your [[Dune/Resource Economy|economy]] online quickly, especially the third [[Dune/Agents and Worker Placement|agent]], then convert that tempo into points.

## Default Priorities

1. Get [[Dune/Swordmaster|Swordmaster]] early if the game state allows it.
2. Build toward a [[Dune/High Council|High Council]] seat if your [[Dune/Deck Building and Card Quality|deck]] wants [[Dune/The Spice Must Flow|The Spice Must Flow]].
3. Add cards that improve [[Dune/Faction Tracks and Alliances|faction access]] or hand quality.
4. Contest only the [[Dune/Combat and Conflicts|conflicts]] that are actually worth the troop spend.
5. Watch which players are racing the same faction tracks or [[Dune/Solari|solari]] spaces.

## Swordmaster

[[Dune/Swordmaster|Swordmaster]] is usually the biggest early tempo upgrade because it gives a third agent for the rest of the game.

Bludgeon's basic opening example uses early **Wealth** and **Secure Contract** turns to reach Swordmaster quickly. In [[Dune/Rise of Ix|Ix]], his opening discussion shows a shipping/solari line that can buy Swordmaster on round 2, with the important bonus that a Swordmaster bought during an agent turn can be used immediately.

Early Swordmaster is strongest when:

- You can reach 8 [[Dune/Solari|solari]] without derailing scoring.
- Your hand can support three meaningful agent turns.
- You are not giving up an immediate point to chase it.

It is weaker when:

- You have no useful icons.
- The table is already moving into point conflicts.
- Your leader or expansion module gives another efficient path.
- The solari path is heavily blocked and a [[Dune/Water|water]], faction, or card-quality opening produces a cleaner next two rounds.

## High Council

[[Dune/High Council|High Council]] is strongest when you expect to buy [[Dune/The Spice Must Flow|The Spice Must Flow]] or expensive cards. It is not automatic; if your deck cannot generate point buys, the seat may be too slow.

## Opening Pivots

Do not force a scripted opener after the table blocks it. In Ix games, if everyone is rushing the same shipping or solari line, Bludgeon's transcript treats water-first development as a real alternative. The right opening is the one that creates a live scoring route by the [[Dune/Game Phase Identification|middle game]].
