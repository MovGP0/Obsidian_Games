# Agents and Worker Placement

Agents are the main action bottleneck.

You start with two agents. Swordmaster gives your third agent and usually changes the whole shape of your turns.

## Agent Turn Rules That Matter Strategically

- You need a card icon to access a matching board space.
- A blocked space may force you into a much weaker action.
- The card used for an agent turn is no longer available for reveal value.
- Sending an agent to a combat space can deploy troops.

## Sequencing

Good sequencing protects both board access and reveal value.

Common questions:

- Which space will be blocked if I wait?
- Which card do I need to keep for reveal?
- Do I need to draw before deciding my final agent turn?
- Can I delay reveal until I see whether the conflict becomes worth contesting?

## Blocking

Blocking is real interaction. Taking a space can deny:

- Solari for Swordmaster or High Council.
- Water for spice access.
- Faction influence thresholds.
- Card draw.
- Combat deployment.
- Expansion-specific spaces like shipping, research, contracts, or worm setup.
