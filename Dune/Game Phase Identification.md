# Game Phase Identification

Dune: Imperium phases are not just round numbers. Identify the phase by the table's conversion pressure: how close players are to turning resources, influence, cards, and troops into victory points.

## Early Game

You are probably in the early game when:

- Most players have 0-3 VP.
- Players still have only two agents.
- Swordmaster and High Council are still being contested or planned.
- Decks still look close to the starting deck.
- Conflicts are mostly resource rewards, not decisive point rewards.
- Faction tracks are below alliance pressure for most players.
- Players are still deciding whether their deck is a persuasion, influence, combat, or hybrid deck.

Main question:

> What engine or access am I building, and how will it score?

Early game priorities:

- Get a third agent if the path is efficient.
- Add faction access and card quality.
- Set up water, spice, solari, or persuasion conversion.
- Avoid overcommitting troops to low-value conflicts.
- Ask whether a setup action has a point route attached. Swordmaster, High Council, and spice are tools, not victory conditions.

## Middle Game

You are probably in the middle game when:

- Players are around 4-7 VP.
- At least one player has Swordmaster or High Council.
- Faction alliances are live or about to become live.
- Conflict rewards are worth serious troop commitments.
- Deck identities are visible.
- Blocking starts denying point routes, not just resources.
- Players are preparing for expensive conversion turns: High Council reveals, faction alliances, tech buys, dreadnought pressure, or Highliner.

Main question:

> Which of my setup lanes is becoming points fastest?

Middle game priorities:

- Convert faction influence into points or alliance pressure.
- Win only conflicts with meaningful rewards.
- Buy cards that support the current plan, not a new unrelated plan.
- Watch opponents' next two likely points.
- Preserve enough troops and spice for the first major point conflict instead of spending them on marginal rewards.

## Late Game

You are probably in the late game when:

- Any player can plausibly reach 10 VP this round or next round.
- The Spice Must Flow is buyable by at least one player.
- Alliances can swing the winner.
- Conflict rewards include direct points or decisive endgame resources.
- Intrigue cards may represent hidden points.
- Engine-building cards are less valuable than immediate conversion.
- Conflict III is active or imminent, which means a single combat can swing multiple points or decisive resources.

Main question:

> Who can end the game, and can I score faster or stop them?

Late game priorities:

- Count visible points and plausible hidden points.
- Deny faction spaces, The Spice Must Flow, or conflict wins when denial changes the winner.
- Spend resources on guaranteed points before speculative engine value.
- Treat every combat deployment as a bid for game position.
- Check whether someone is holding enough spice for Highliner or enough persuasion for The Spice Must Flow before you spend your blocking action elsewhere.

## Quick Test

If you are unsure, ask:

1. Are players still mainly buying infrastructure? Early.
2. Are players converting engines into points but not ending yet? Middle.
3. Can someone reach 10 now or next round? Late.
