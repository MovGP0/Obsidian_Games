# Leader Selection Before Game Start

Leader selection is the first strategic decision. Bludgeon's leader-ranking and leader-guide transcripts repeatedly treat leaders as route-shapers, not just power ratings.

## What To Evaluate

- What does the leader make cheaper, faster, or more reliable?
- Does the signet ring create resources, influence, cards, or combat pressure?
- Does the leader need specific board spaces?
- Does the leader need specific cards from the Imperium row?
- Does the leader improve a known scoring lane or only create flexible value?
- Does the leader change in [[Dune/Rise of Ix|Ix]], [[Dune/Immortality|Immortality]], or [[Dune/Uprising|Uprising]]?

## Pick For The Table

Before choosing, look at:

- Player order.
- Visible Imperium row.
- First conflict.
- Expansion modules.
- Whether opponents are likely to contest the same spaces.

A leader that is strong in a vacuum can be awkward if the row, turn order, or module denies their natural route.

## Route Examples

- Solari leader: easier [[Dune/Swordmaster|Swordmaster]], [[Dune/High Council|High Council]], or Mentat lines.
- Spice leader: stronger [[Dune/Highliner|Highliner]], tech, or expensive card-effect lines.
- Faction leader: faster [[Dune/Faction Tracks and Alliances|influence]] points and alliance threats.
- Deck leader: stronger trashing, draw, or [[Dune/Persuasion|persuasion]] conversion.
- Combat leader: better conflict timing and troop leverage.
