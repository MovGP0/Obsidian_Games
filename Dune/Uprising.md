# Uprising

Dune: Imperium - Uprising is a standalone game in the Dune: Imperium system, not just a small expansion.

## What Changes

- New board.
- New Imperium and Intrigue decks.
- Spies.
- Contracts.
- Objectives.
- Worms and Maker Hooks.
- Revised board-space incentives.

## Strategic Shift

Uprising increases the importance of tactical positioning and explosive conflict turns. Worms can double conflict rewards, so combat math changes sharply once Maker Hooks are in play.

## Spies

Spies create delayed flexibility. Each player starts with three spies, and spies are placed on observation posts.

Key spy checks:

- A spy icon places a spy on an empty observation post.
- Infiltrate recalls a connected spy to access an occupied connected space.
- Spy-agent icons can access connected spaces without recalling the spy.
- Recall-spy effects pull a spy back from the board to your supply.
- One spy cannot pay multiple costs at once, so choose whether the spy is for access, information, or a recall effect.

This makes spy timing a board-control question. A spy placed too early may reveal your plan; a spy held too long may fail to unlock the occupied space you needed.

## Contracts

Contracts add side objectives and change the value of several spaces. A contract is not just a small bonus; it can reshape the route to Swordmaster, High Council, spies, troops, water, solari, and card draw.

Completed contracts can also matter later because some cards care about how many contracts you have finished.

## Worms

Worms make conflict valuation more volatile. A player with worm access may turn a normal conflict into a game-shaping reward.

Bludgeon's primer highlights three practical checks:

- You usually need Maker Hooks before a worm turn is live.
- Worms add combat strength and can double the conflict reward.
- Shield Wall and board state can prevent or enable the worm line.

Do not evaluate a Uprising conflict by troop count alone. A player with hooks and access to worm spaces may threaten a doubled reward with fewer troops than expected.

## Battle Icons

Uprising's battle icons add another layer to conflict tracking. Winning first place in the right conflict can award battle icons, and objective cards can also provide them.

Track battle icons publicly. They can change which future rewards or objectives are live and make a player more dangerous even when their current VP total looks ordinary.

## Board Changes

Several familiar spaces have new incentives:

- High Council has the familiar role but can provide extra value when visited.
- Swordmaster costs 8 solari for the first player and 6 solari for later players.
- Mentat is replaced by Imperial Spy, changing the short-term extra-agent pattern.
- CHOAM and contract spaces create economy lines that do not map cleanly to the base game.

## Practical Notes

- Do not evaluate combat as if it were base Dune: Imperium.
- Watch Maker Hooks timing.
- Contracts can create point and economy lines outside the base-game patterns.
- Objectives create additional pressure around board control and timing.
- In Uprising plus Ix, some Ix spaces are adapted around contracts, so do not assume the base Ix board flow applies unchanged.
- Bludgeon rates Bloodlines as the cleanest expansion fit for Uprising in his primer.

## Bludgeon Primer Checklist

Bludgeon's Uprising primer frames the new material as a set of systems to learn:

- Spies: placement, infiltrate, gather intelligence, spy icons, and recall effects.
- Contracts: what kind of contract is available and whether your deck naturally completes it.
- Sandworms: whether you can summon them and whether the current reward is worth doubling.
- Maker Hooks: whether you have the hook before the turn where worms matter.
- Shield Wall: whether it blocks the worm line and who can remove it.
- Battle icons: whether card factions and battle-icon rules change combat math.
- Objectives: whether the objective points change normal board-space priority.
- New board spaces: whether a familiar base-game habit is now weaker or stronger.

The practical takeaway is to evaluate each Uprising round by the active module pressure. Some rounds are spy rounds, some are contract rounds, and some are worm-combat rounds.
