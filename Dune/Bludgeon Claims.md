## Claims compared

| Bludgeon claim | Assessment |
|---|---|
| "The correct alliance is ... Atreides and Bene Gesserit." | **High synergy, overstated absolutism.** Strong pair; not always the correct pair. |
| "With merely three cards you can win almost any leader to leader duel ..." | **Conceptually right, literally overstated.** Treat as a threat profile, not a guarantee. |
| Bene Gesserit has "strong combat ability combined with the ability to avoid combat ..." | **Agree in spirit; strongest in advanced play, only partial in strict basic.** |

## Takeaway

Bludgeon's advice is useful but rhetorically strong. Keep the strategic insight and remove the universal wording.

Atreides plus Bene Gesserit is a serious alliance. Bene Gesserit is scary because it changes combat incentives. But Dune remains a board-state game, not a slogan game.
