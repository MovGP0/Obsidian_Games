# Stage Priorities

| Stage | Priorities |
|---|---|
| Early | Protect your mobility anchor, buy or threaten useful cards, avoid vanity battles, map likely spice and ally lanes |
| Mid | Convert hand quality into selective fights, form the alliance that fixes your bottleneck, punish overextension, block easy thirds |
| Late | Count every reachable stronghold, value denial over savings, respect Guild and Fremen end states, and ask who still moves after you |

## Early game

Focus on:

- Spice collection.
- Getting useful treachery cards.
- Avoiding costly fights.
- Understanding player intentions.
- Forming temporary non-aggression deals.
- Protecting a mobility city if your faction depends on one.
- Mapping likely ally lanes.

Do not rush strongholds unless the opening is genuinely cheap.

## Mid game

Focus on:

- Positioning near strongholds.
- Identifying the leading player or alliance.
- Building a credible threat.
- Preserving enough troops for a final push.
- Forcing opponents to spend resources.
- Converting hand quality into selective fights.
- Forming an alliance that solves your current bottleneck.

This is where the political game intensifies.

## Late game

Focus on:

- Exact stronghold counts.
- Who moves before whom.
- Whether an alliance can win this turn.
- Who can block whom.
- Whether you can win now.
- Whether you need to stop someone else before pursuing your own plan.
- Whether Guild or Fremen endgame pressure is being underestimated.

Late-game Dune is often about preventing someone else's win before executing your own.
