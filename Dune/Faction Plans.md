# Faction Plans

## Summary table

| Faction | Core abilities and identity | Main strengths | Main weaknesses | Recommended playstyle |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [[Dune/Atreides|Atreides]] | Bidding prescience, top spice-deck peek before movement, one-element battle peek, early Arrakeen start, movement-3 access if city held | Information economy, strong positional flexibility, high-quality battle decisions | Spice-poor relative to needs; must both buy cards and ship forces | **Defensive-information broker early; precise striker midgame; opportunist finisher late** |
| [[Dune/Harkonnen|Harkonnen]] | Keep all drawn traitors, hand limit 8, buy one get one card, Carthag start | Threat density, bluff pressure, strong opening volatility | Income can collapse if overbidding or overfighting | **Front-load pressure, buy aggressively but not ruinously, pick fights that force bad leader/card usage** |
| [[Dune/Emperor|Emperor]] | Gets other players' bidding payments, large opening bankroll | Stable economy, can underwrite alliance plans, strong city play | Too easy to become rich and passive; visible board targets | **Convert money into board leverage, not hoarding; hold at least one mobility city and fund decisive shipments** |
| [[Dune/Spacing Guild|Spacing Guild]] | Paid for others' shipments, flexible shipping types, cheap shipping, special default win if nobody wins by game end | Tempo control, map-wide repositioning, best denial faction | Weak leaders, slower card timing early, can look weak before round timing matters | **Play spoiler-controller first, kingmaker second, default-winner always** |
| [[Dune/Fremen|Fremen]] | Free deployment near Great Flat, 2-space movement, worm-riding, alternative endgame win if the board stalls | Best natural desert mobility, low cash deployment cost, denial power in long games | Low starting spice, poor early bidding position, prone to under-equipment in base play | **Patience first, board spread second, selective strike and denial finish** |
| [[Dune/Bene Gesserit|Bene Gesserit]] | Prediction, free shadow shipping into Polar Sink when others arrive from orbit, Voice in battle | Table influence, battle control, political leverage, alliance desirability | Slow revival, can become too passive; in pure basic game less board-evasive than advanced reputation suggests | **Manipulate pace, avoid fair attrition, win via timing, alliance leverage, or prediction pressure** |
